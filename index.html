Magna Carta of Women to Anti-VAWC & beyond: policy frameworks, lived realities, and the road to equality

Pillars of Gender Justice

RA 9710 – Magna Carta of Women

Comprehensive rights and protections for women. Promotes gender equality and empowerment.

RA 6725 – Anti-Discrimination in Employment

Protects workers from discrimination based on sex and civil status.

RA 7600 – Breastfeeding Act

Supports breastfeeding mothers through workplace protection and lactation stations.

RA 9262 – Anti-VAWC Act

Protects women and children from abuse and violence in relationships.

More Laws: Protection & Participation

RA 7688 – Women in Military

Ensures equal military opportunities for women.

RA 7192 – Women in Development Act

Promotes women participation in economic and social development.

RA 10354 – RH Law

Provides access to reproductive healthcare and family planning.

RA 8353 – Anti-Rape Law

Strengthens protection for survivors of sexual assault and marital rape.

Workplace Protections

RA 7877 – Anti-Sexual Harassment Act

  • Prohibits sexual harassment in work and schools
  • Employers must create grievance systems
  • Promotes safe environments for everyone

Critical Reflection

How do these laws promote gender equality?

  • They protect equal rights and opportunities for women and LGBTQIA+ individuals.
  • They reduce discrimination in schools, workplaces, and communities.
  • They provide legal remedies for victims of abuse, harassment, and violence.
  • They encourage equal participation in leadership, employment, and public service.
  • They strengthen access to reproductive health, education, and workplace protection.

Gains & Achievements

  • Stronger legal protection for women and children.
  • Improved workplace safety and anti-harassment policies.
  • Better awareness about gender sensitivity and human rights.
  • Expanded access to reproductive healthcare and maternal services.
  • Creation of VAWC desks and gender programs in communities.

Remaining Issues & Gaps

  • Weak implementation of laws in some barangays and provinces.
  • Victim-blaming and harmful stereotypes still exist.
  • Some victims are afraid to report abuse due to fear and stigma.
  • Limited access to gender services in rural communities.
  • LGBTQIA+ discrimination continues because the SOGIE Equality Bill is still pending.

Why do these laws matter?

These laws establish accountability, protect human rights, and help create safer environments. They also encourage equality, dignity, and respect for all genders.

Key Messages

  • Know the laws and your rights
  • Support survivors and victims
  • Promote equality and inclusivity
  • Help stop discrimination and violence
